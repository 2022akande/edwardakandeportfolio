About Me

Hello, my name is Edward Akande and I am a full-stack web developer.
I got my first computer when I was 9, the tower itself was bigger than my chest
and just about too heavy. Ever since then I was fascinated with computer science.
Over the years that fascination grew and became hunger, I began learning everything I could
get in front of me. It is estimated that there is a little under 2 billion active
websites, this one included. Who's visiting these websites? What kind of websites are they
visiting? How does one make a website? These are the questions that led me and many
others down the path of web development.

Time for some question and answer

Q: Do you prefer to work alone or on a team?

A: A team. There are many pros to working on a team the most obvious being a lighter workload.
In addition to less work, you're also exposed to other peoples code, meaning you might pick up some
of their good habits. Every developer out there has something to teach me whether they've been coding
8 years or 8 months.


Q: What web browser do you use?

A: All of them. As a web developer, I have to see how my code interacts with any web browser
a user might find themselves on.


Q: What is your favorite programing language?

A: Don't have one. I haven't learned all of them yet so how could I possibly know which one I like
the most? Don't worry I'll keep you posted. As for the ones I know now python has certainly proved to be
more versatile than I expected.


Q: Which websites do you find annoying or flawed

A: Don't even get me started with Netflix, who told them I wanted to hear a deafening clip of every movie
I pass over on my way to what I want to watch? In my opinion, any noise that occurs outside of a
media player is unnecessary.
